Connecting Players to Content They'll Love at Rec Room, Inc.

UI Engineering

At Rec Room, I helped deliver UI improvements backed by A/B testing and analytics. I helped improve the cross-platform game menus, including localizing text, showing more data in the UI for discovery, and freshening up content layouts.

* Enabled official support for eight languages by using proprietary and Unity localization tools to prepare in-game text for a third-party localization service, strengthening the game's presence in the existing markets.

* Improved time spent and room visits by X.X% for new players by reducing vertical and horizontal scroll to just vertical scroll in the main content menu, which was part of a UI simplification experiment.

* Improved first-party and UGC playable content discovery by implementing a doubled-height content row; increased impressions by X.X% and click-throughs by X.X%.

* Shipped other experiments and improvements: one to improve the experience of inviting friends to a session, if the two players are on different versions; one to experiment on notification frequency for mobile phones; one to add a zoom button to the avatar hat selection screen.

* Fixed bugs in support of a project by the Lead UI Engineer; their project significantly sped up rendering on VR headsets and enabled more flexible layouts.

* Fixed bugs in support of content discovery features in the player's personalizable room and in a new player discovery flow; my teammates' features reduced cognitive load and increased impressions and click-throughs.

* Shipped several content discovery experiments that validated the control group, which gave my product leads useful metrics to design follow-up experiments.
